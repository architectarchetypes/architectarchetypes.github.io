The “architect” is a story of fiction.

Our project reframes the history of architects representing themselves through the medium of lectures. In turn, we replace the notion of the neutral or circumstantial architect “persona,” with one that is curated and reinforced itself through “jargon.” Through extracting language used from this semester’s MIT SA+P lecture series and Fall 2022 Lectures at Harvard GSD, Columbia GSAPP, Princeton SoA, and Yale Architecture, we plan to reveal how contemporary architectural practitioners and thinkers portray themselves and whether it hinders or helps the advancement of the discipline.
